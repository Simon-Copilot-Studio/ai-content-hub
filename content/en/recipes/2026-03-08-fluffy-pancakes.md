Start your morning right with these heavenly fluffy pancakes that melt in your mouth. Made from simple pantry ingredients, this recipe produces light, tender, and golden brown pancakes every single time.

## Why This Recipe Works

The secret to perfect pancakes lies in restraint. By combining dry and wet ingredients separately and then **gently folding** them together, you avoid developing gluten — the protein that makes pancakes tough and chewy instead of tender.

Three rules that guarantee fluffy pancakes:
1. **Don't overmix** — lumps are your friend
2. **Rest the batter** — let baking powder activate
3. **Medium heat only** — too hot burns the outside before the inside cooks

## The Technique That Changes Everything

### Separate, Then Fold

Whisk all dry ingredients in one bowl, all wet in another. Pour wet into dry and fold with a spatula — not a whisk — using no more than 15-20 strokes. The batter should look rough and lumpy. If it's smooth, you've gone too far.

### Rest the Batter

After mixing, walk away for 5-10 minutes. During this time, the baking powder creates tiny air bubbles throughout the batter, and the flour hydrates fully. The batter will look slightly puffier when you return.

### The Bubble Test

Pour batter onto a properly heated griddle and watch the surface. When bubbles form across the entire surface and the edges look dry and set (about 2-3 minutes), it's time to flip. Only flip once — pressing down or flipping multiple times deflates those precious air pockets.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Using cold ingredients:** Cold milk and eggs create denser pancakes. Let them come to room temperature, or warm the milk slightly in the microwave.

**Pressing with the spatula:** It's tempting, but pressing down on pancakes squeezes out air and makes them flat and dense.

**Crowding the pan:** Leave at least 2 inches between pancakes. They need room to spread and cook evenly.

## Variations

- **Blueberry pancakes:** Fold 1/2 cup fresh blueberries into the batter
- **Chocolate chip:** Add 1/3 cup mini chocolate chips
- **Banana:** Mash 1 ripe banana into the wet ingredients
- **Whole wheat:** Replace half the flour with whole wheat flour
- **Buttermilk:** Substitute buttermilk for milk and add 1/2 tsp baking soda

## Serving Suggestions

**Classic:** Stack tall with a pat of butter and warm maple syrup.

**Fruit lover:** Top with fresh strawberries, blueberries, and a drizzle of honey.

**Indulgent:** Spread with Nutella, add sliced bananas, and dust with powdered sugar.

**Savory twist:** Skip the sugar in the batter, add shredded cheese, and serve with bacon and eggs.

## Storage and Reheating

Leftover pancakes freeze beautifully. Cool completely, stack with parchment paper between each pancake, and freeze in a zip-top bag for up to 2 months. Reheat in a toaster or 350°F oven for 5 minutes — they'll taste freshly made.
