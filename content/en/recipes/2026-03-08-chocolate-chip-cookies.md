There's a science to the perfect chocolate chip cookie. Crispy edges, chewy centers, and pools of melted chocolate — this isn't luck, it's technique. Once you understand the *why* behind each step, you'll nail it every single time.

## The Sugar Ratio

This is where cookie texture is decided:

- **Brown sugar** = moisture + chew (molasses content absorbs moisture)
- **White sugar** = crispness + spread (promotes spreading and browning)

Equal parts creates the perfect balance: crispy edges that shatter, with soft, chewy centers that bend. Want chewier? More brown sugar. Want crispier? More white sugar.

## Butter Temperature Matters

Softened butter — not melted, not cold. It should hold an indent when pressed but not be greasy or shiny. Why?

- **Cold butter** doesn't cream properly (dense, flat cookies)
- **Melted butter** creates chewier but flatter cookies (no air incorporation)
- **Softened butter** incorporates air during creaming, giving cookies lift and that ideal texture

## The Chill Factor

Chilling the dough for at least 30 minutes does three things:

1. **Prevents spreading** — cold dough takes longer to melt in the oven, so cookies set before they spread thin
2. **Develops flavor** — flour hydrates fully, and the sugars create deeper caramel notes
3. **Better texture** — cold dough creates a taller cookie with thicker, chewier centers

For the absolute best cookies, chill 24 hours. The flavor difference is remarkable.

## The Baking Sweet Spot

375°F for 9-11 minutes. Pull them when the edges are golden but the centers still look slightly underdone and shiny. They'll continue baking on the hot sheet for another 2-3 minutes after you remove them from the oven.

**The biggest mistake home bakers make:** Baking until the centers *look* done. By that point, they're overbaked. Trust the carryover cooking.

## Chocolate Choice

- **Chips** — hold their shape, familiar look, uniform melting
- **Chunks** (chopped from a bar) — irregular shapes, some melt into pools while others stay intact, more artisanal look
- **Mix of dark and milk chocolate** — complexity and variety in every bite

## Storage

Airtight container at room temperature: 5 days. Frozen dough balls: 3 months (bake from frozen, add 1-2 minutes). Frozen baked cookies: 2 months (microwave 10 seconds to refresh).
