Avocado toast has become a breakfast staple for good reason — it's delicious, nutritious, and incredibly versatile. But not all avocado toast is created equal. This recipe takes the simple concept and elevates it to restaurant quality.

## Why This Recipe Works

The secret lies in the details: perfectly ripe avocados, bread that won't go soggy, and thoughtful toppings that complement rather than overwhelm. Each bite offers creamy avocado against crunchy bread, sweet tomatoes against salty feta, and fresh herbs against rich olive oil.

## The Art of Choosing Avocados

Look for avocados that yield slightly to gentle pressure but aren't mushy. They should be dark green to black in color. If they're too firm, place them in a paper bag with a banana at room temperature for 1-2 days.

**The lemon juice trick:** Beyond adding brightness, lemon juice prevents the avocado from oxidizing and turning brown. Don't skip it.

## Bread Matters More Than You Think

Use good quality bread with structure — sourdough, rye, or whole grain work best. The bread needs to support the weight of the toppings without becoming soggy. Toast until golden and crisp, then rub with garlic while still warm for an extra layer of flavor.

## Blistered Tomatoes: The Upgrade

Instead of raw cherry tomatoes, a quick 2-3 minute blister in a hot skillet transforms them. The heat concentrates their sweetness and creates a burst-in-your-mouth texture that's leagues ahead of cold tomatoes.

## Assembly Tips

- **Don't over-mash** — keeping the avocado slightly chunky adds texture
- **Layer strategically** — avocado first (it acts as glue), then tomatoes, then cheese
- **Drizzle last** — olive oil and balsamic glaze on top for visual appeal and flavor

## Variations

- **Protein boost:** Add a poached egg, smoked salmon, or crispy bacon
- **Spice it up:** Swap red pepper flakes for Everything Bagel seasoning
- **Go vegan:** Replace feta with nutritional yeast and hemp seeds
- **Mediterranean max:** Add sliced cucumber, Kalamata olives, and za'atar
