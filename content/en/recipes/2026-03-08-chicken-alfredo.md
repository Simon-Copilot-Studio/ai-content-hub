Alfredo pasta is one of those dishes that people think they need a restaurant for. They don't. With good Parmesan, real cream, and 30 minutes, you can make a version that's better than most Italian-American restaurants serve.

## The Real Alfredo Secret

Authentic Alfredo is just butter, Parmesan, and pasta water. The Italian-American version adds cream, which is admittedly less authentic but creates a richer, more forgiving sauce. Either way, the secret is the same: **freshly grated Parmesan from a block**.

Pre-grated Parmesan contains cellulose (anti-caking powder) that prevents smooth melting. The difference between block-grated and pre-grated is the difference between a silky sauce and a grainy one. This is non-negotiable.

## Building the Sauce

### The Cream Base

Heavy cream, not milk, not half-and-half. You need at least 36% fat content to create a sauce that clings to pasta without breaking. Bring it to a gentle simmer — never a full boil, or it can separate.

### The Parmesan Integration

Off low heat, whisk in the Parmesan slowly. Cold cheese into hot cream creates an emulsion. If you dump it all at once, you get clumps. Add in three batches, whisking between each, until the sauce is smooth and glossy.

### The Nutmeg

A quarter teaspoon. You won't taste nutmeg specifically, but you'll taste *something missing* if you leave it out. It rounds out the richness and adds a subtle warmth that elevates the entire dish.

## Pasta Water: The Secret Ingredient

Reserve at least 1 cup of starchy pasta water before draining. This water is gold — it thickens the sauce, helps it cling to the pasta, and adds body without diluting flavor. Add it a tablespoon at a time until you reach the perfect consistency.

## The Chicken

Season simply — salt and pepper are enough. Sear over medium-high heat until golden. The fond (brown bits) left in the pan becomes the flavor base for the garlic and cream. Don't skip this step by boiling or steaming the chicken.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Boiling the cream** — causes separation
2. **Using pre-grated Parmesan** — grainy sauce guaranteed
3. **Letting it sit** — Alfredo thickens rapidly; serve immediately
4. **Overcooking the garlic** — 30 seconds maximum, or it turns bitter

## Make It a Complete Meal

- Add sautéed mushrooms and spinach
- Top with crispy pancetta or bacon
- Serve with garlic bread and a simple arugula salad
