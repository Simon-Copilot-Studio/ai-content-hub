Korean fried chicken has conquered the world for one reason: that impossibly thin, shatteringly crisp coating that stays crunchy even when drenched in sauce. American fried chicken has thick, bready breading. Korean fried chicken has a glass-like shell that shatters when you bite through it.

## The Double-Fry Technique

This is the secret that separates good fried chicken from legendary fried chicken.

### First Fry (325°F, 6-8 minutes)

The lower temperature cooks the chicken through gently while creating an initial crust. The batter — a thin mixture of flour, cornstarch, and ice water — forms a delicate shell around the meat.

### The Rest (10 minutes)

This step is crucial. As the chicken cools, moisture from the meat migrates to the surface, softening the crust slightly. This sets up the second fry.

### Second Fry (375°F, 2-3 minutes)

The higher temperature blasts away all that surface moisture, rendering out excess fat from the crust and creating an ultra-thin, ultra-crispy shell. The result is a coating that's more like tempered glass than breading.

## The Gochujang Sauce

Gochujang is fermented Korean chili paste — it's spicy, sweet, and deeply savory all at once. Combined with honey, soy sauce, and rice vinegar, it creates a glaze that clings to the fried chicken without making it soggy (thanks to that bulletproof double-fried crust).

### Heat Level

Gochujang varies in spiciness by brand. Start with the amount listed and taste before tossing. For extra heat, add gochugaru (Korean chili flakes). For milder, increase the honey.

## Why Cornstarch Matters

The 2:1 flour-to-cornstarch ratio is key. Cornstarch creates a lighter, crispier crust than flour alone because it contains no gluten. The baking powder adds tiny air pockets that make the crust even lighter and crunchier.

## Ice Water = Crispier Crust

Cold batter creates a bigger temperature differential when it hits hot oil, which means more steam, which means crispier results. Always use ice water, never room temperature.

## Serving Style

Korean fried chicken is traditionally served as:
- **Anju** (drinking snack) — with beer or soju
- **With pickled radish** — the acidity cuts through the richness
- **Over rice** — drizzle extra sauce for a complete meal
