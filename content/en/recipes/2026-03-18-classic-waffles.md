Waffles are the ultimate weekend breakfast — crispy on the outside, fluffy on the inside, and endlessly customizable. This recipe delivers restaurant-quality waffles every single time.

## The Secret to Perfect Waffles

Three things separate great waffles from mediocre ones:

1. **Separated eggs** — Folding whipped egg whites into the batter creates an incredibly light, airy interior
2. **Don't overmix** — A few lumps in the batter are your friend. Overmixing develops gluten and makes tough waffles
3. **Hot iron, patience** — Let the waffle cook until steam stops escaping. Opening too early = soggy waffles

## Tips for the Crispiest Exterior

- Use melted butter (not oil) for better flavor and crunch
- A tablespoon of cornstarch mixed into the flour adds extra crispiness
- Let the batter rest 5 minutes before cooking — this hydrates the flour

## Topping Ideas

- **Classic**: Maple syrup + butter
- **Berry bliss**: Mixed berries + whipped cream + powdered sugar
- **Savory twist**: Fried chicken + hot honey + pickles
- **Chocolate lover**: Nutella + sliced bananas + chopped hazelnuts
- **Tropical**: Coconut whipped cream + mango + toasted coconut flakes

## Storage

Leftover waffles freeze beautifully. Place cooled waffles in a single layer on a baking sheet, freeze for 1 hour, then transfer to a zip-lock bag. They'll keep for up to 3 months.
