This is the fastest impressive dinner you can make. Fifteen minutes from cold pan to plated, and it tastes like something from a seafood restaurant. The combination of butter, garlic, and lemon with perfectly seared shrimp is one of cooking's simplest and most satisfying formulas.

## The Secret: Don't Touch the Shrimp

The biggest mistake with shrimp is fussing with them. Place them in a single layer on a hot, buttery pan and **leave them alone for 90 seconds**. You want golden-brown caramelization on the bottom before you flip. Constantly stirring gives you steamed, gray shrimp instead of seared, golden shrimp.

## Dry = Sear, Wet = Steam

Pat every single shrimp dry with paper towels. This is the step people skip, and it's the reason their shrimp don't get that restaurant-quality sear. Surface moisture creates steam, which prevents the Maillard reaction (browning) from occurring.

## Building the Sauce

After removing the seared shrimp, you have a pan full of fond (those golden-brown bits) and butter. This is pure concentrated shrimp flavor. Add fresh butter, then garlic. The garlic cooks in 30-60 seconds — watch it carefully. Burned garlic is bitter and ruins the dish.

The lemon juice goes in last, off heat, to preserve its brightness. Hot lemon juice loses its zing.

## Don't Overcrowd the Pan

If your shrimp are touching each other, you've overcrowded the pan. The temperature drops, the shrimp release liquid, and you end up braising instead of searing. Work in two batches if needed — it only adds 2 minutes.

## Shrimp Size Guide

- **21-25 count** (large): ideal for this recipe — substantial, easy to sear
- **16-20 count** (extra-large): even better sear, slightly longer cooking
- **26-30 count** (medium): works but sears too fast, harder to get the timing right

## Serving Ideas

- **Over pasta** — toss with linguine for shrimp scampi
- **With rice** — jasmine or basmati, let the sauce soak in
- **In tacos** — with slaw and avocado crema
- **As an appetizer** — with crusty bread for sauce-soaking
- **Over salad** — warm shrimp on cold greens with lemon vinaigrette
