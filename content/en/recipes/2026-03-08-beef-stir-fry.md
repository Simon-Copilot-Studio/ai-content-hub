A good stir fry is the fastest route to a complete, satisfying dinner. Fifteen minutes of prep, five minutes of cooking, and you have something that beats takeout in both flavor and nutrition. The secret is preparation — have everything cut and measured before the wok gets hot, because once you start cooking, things move fast.

## Mise en Place Is Non-Negotiable

Stir frying happens at extreme speed. Once the oil starts smoking, you have about 5 minutes total. There's no time to chop garlic or measure soy sauce. Cut all your vegetables, make the sauce, and line everything up in order of addition. This isn't optional — it's the difference between great stir fry and a burnt mess.

## Cutting the Beef

**Against the grain, thin.** Look at the beef and find the direction of the muscle fibers (they look like parallel lines). Cut perpendicular to those lines. This shortens the fibers, resulting in tender pieces that don't require chewing. Aim for 1/4 inch thick slices.

**Freezing trick:** Pop the beef in the freezer for 20 minutes before slicing. Partially frozen beef is dramatically easier to slice thin.

## The Velveting Technique

Tossing the beef with cornstarch and a splash of soy sauce before cooking is a Chinese restaurant technique called "velveting." The cornstarch creates a thin protective coating that:
- Prevents moisture loss during high-heat cooking
- Creates a silky texture on the surface
- Helps the sauce cling to the meat later

## The Wok Rules

### Heat
The wok must be **smoking hot** before anything goes in. High heat is what creates "wok hei" — that smoky, charred flavor that makes restaurant stir fry taste different from home cooking.

### Don't Stir Too Much
Despite the name "stir fry," constant stirring prevents browning. Let the beef sit for 30-45 seconds to develop a crust before tossing.

### Cook in Stages
Hard vegetables first (carrots, broccoli — 2-3 minutes), then soft vegetables (peppers, snap peas — 2 minutes), then aromatics (garlic, ginger — 30 seconds). This ensures everything is perfectly cooked.

## The Sauce Formula

The base ratio for any stir fry sauce: **soy sauce + oyster sauce + cornstarch + something sweet + something acidic**. This recipe uses honey and rice vinegar, but you can substitute brown sugar and lime juice, or maple syrup and mirin. The cornstarch thickens the sauce into a glossy coating that clings to everything.

## Protein Swaps

- **Chicken thigh** — cut thin, same velveting technique
- **Pork tenderloin** — sliced thin, cooks slightly faster
- **Shrimp** — no velveting needed, cook until pink
- **Tofu** — extra-firm, pressed, cubed, sear until golden before adding vegetables
