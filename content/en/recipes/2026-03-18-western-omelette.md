The Western omelette (also called a Denver omelette) is a diner classic that's surprisingly easy to master at home. It's hearty, protein-packed, and ready in under 15 minutes.

## Getting the Perfect Fold

The biggest challenge with omelettes is the fold. Here's the trick: cook on medium heat (never high!), and fold when the top is still slightly wet. Residual heat finishes the cooking, giving you a creamy interior.

## The Right Pan Matters

A 10-inch non-stick skillet is ideal for a 3-egg omelette. Too big and the eggs spread too thin; too small and it won't cook evenly.

## Variations

- **Greek**: Feta, spinach, sun-dried tomatoes, and olives
- **Mushroom Swiss**: Sautéed mushrooms with Swiss cheese
- **Mexican**: Chorizo, jalapeños, pepper jack, and salsa
- **Garden**: Zucchini, tomato, basil, and goat cheese
