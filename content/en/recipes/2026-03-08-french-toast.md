French toast is more than breakfast — it's a warm, comforting embrace on a plate. Golden-brown crust giving way to soft, custard-infused interior that practically melts in your mouth. This recipe gets it right every time.

## The Bread Makes Everything

Not all bread is created equal for French toast. You need something with structure and substance:

**Best choices:**
- **Brioche** — rich, buttery, and slightly sweet. The gold standard.
- **Challah** — eggy and tender with a tight crumb
- **Texas toast** — thick-cut and sturdy

**Avoid:** Thin sandwich bread, anything with large air holes (like ciabatta), or very fresh soft bread that will fall apart when soaked.

**Pro tip:** Day-old bread is *better* than fresh. The slight drying allows it to absorb more custard without becoming mushy.

## The Custard Ratio

The perfect ratio is **1 egg per 2 slices of bread**, with enough milk to make the mixture pourable but not watery. Too much milk and the toast becomes soggy. Too little and it won't penetrate the bread.

### The Soak

Thirty to forty-five seconds per side — no more. You want the custard to penetrate about halfway through the bread, leaving the center slightly dry. This creates the ideal texture: custardy edges with a tender, bread-like center.

## Temperature Is Everything

**Medium-low heat**. This is non-negotiable.

High heat burns the outside before the custard sets inside, leaving you with charred edges and raw, eggy centers. Low and slow allows the sugars to caramelize gradually, creating that perfect golden color and crisp surface.

## The Cinnamon Question

A little cinnamon goes a long way. Half a teaspoon for the entire batch is plenty — you want warmth, not a cinnamon roll. The nutmeg adds a subtle depth that most people can't identify but would miss if it were gone.

## Keeping Warm

The first pieces get cold while you cook the rest. Solution: place finished slices on a wire rack set over a baking sheet in a 200°F oven. The wire rack prevents the bottoms from getting soggy — stacking directly on a plate traps steam.

## Serving Ideas

- **Classic:** Butter, warm maple syrup, powdered sugar
- **Berry:** Fresh strawberries, blueberries, whipped cream
- **Decadent:** Nutella spread, sliced bananas, toasted pecans
- **Savory twist:** Skip the sugar, add herbs, serve with bacon and cheese
