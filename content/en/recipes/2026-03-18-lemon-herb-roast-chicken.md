A perfectly roasted chicken is one of the most satisfying things you can cook. Golden, crackling skin giving way to juicy, herb-infused meat — it's simple, elegant, and feeds a crowd.

## The Dry Chicken Secret

The single most important step? **Dry the chicken thoroughly.** Moisture is the enemy of crispy skin. Pat it down with paper towels, and for even better results, leave it uncovered in the fridge overnight. The cold air dries the skin beautifully.

## Butter Under the Skin

Spreading herb butter directly under the skin does two things: it bastes the meat from the inside as it melts, and it creates an incredibly flavorful, crispy skin. Don't skip this step.

## Resting Is Non-Negotiable

After roasting, let the chicken rest at least 15 minutes before carving. This allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat. Cut too early and all that moisture runs onto your cutting board instead of staying in the chicken.

## Pan Sauce Bonus

While the chicken rests, place the roasting pan on the stovetop over medium heat. Add 1/2 cup white wine or chicken broth, scraping up the browned bits. Simmer until slightly reduced. Strain and serve alongside the chicken — it's liquid gold.

## Leftover Ideas

- Chicken salad sandwiches
- Chicken tacos with leftover meat
- Homemade chicken stock from the carcass
- Fried rice with shredded leftovers
