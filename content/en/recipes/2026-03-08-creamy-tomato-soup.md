This isn't your average tomato soup. By roasting fresh tomatoes first and adding cream cheese as a secret ingredient, you get a depth of flavor and velvety richness that canned soup can never match.

## Why Roasting Changes Everything

Most tomato soups use canned tomatoes dumped straight into a pot. That works, but roasting fresh tomatoes at high heat for 25-30 minutes does something magical: it caramelizes the natural sugars, concentrates the flavor, and removes excess water. The result is a soup that tastes like tomatoes turned up to eleven.

## The Secret Ingredient: Cream Cheese

Heavy cream makes tomato soup smooth. But cream cheese makes it *luxurious*. Four ounces of softened cream cheese, whisked in at the end, gives the soup a body and richness that cream alone can't achieve. It also adds a subtle tang that complements the roasted tomatoes beautifully.

## Building Layers of Flavor

### The Roast (25 minutes)

Cut tomatoes in half, arrange cut-side up, drizzle with olive oil. High heat, minimal fuss. You'll know they're ready when the edges start to char and the kitchen smells incredible.

### The Sauté (7 minutes)

While tomatoes roast, sauté onion and garlic in olive oil. Then add tomato paste — this concentrated paste adds umami depth that balances the sweetness of the roasted tomatoes.

### The Simmer (15 minutes)

Combine everything with broth and herbs. A teaspoon of sugar isn't about making the soup sweet — it's about neutralizing the natural acidity that can make tomato soup taste sharp.

### The Blend

An immersion blender is ideal — blend right in the pot, no mess. For the smoothest possible texture, strain through a fine-mesh sieve after blending.

### The Finish

Off-heat, whisk in cream and cream cheese until completely smooth. Return to gentle heat — never boil after adding dairy, or it may curdle.

## The Perfect Pairing

Tomato soup and grilled cheese is one of civilization's greatest food combinations. The crispy, buttery bread and melted cheese against the smooth, tangy soup creates a contrast of temperatures and textures that's almost impossible to improve upon.

For a grown-up version: use sourdough bread, gruyère cheese, and add a thin layer of fig jam inside the sandwich.

## Make It Ahead

This soup improves with time. Make it a day ahead and reheat gently — the flavors meld and deepen overnight. It freezes well for up to 3 months (freeze before adding cream; stir cream in when reheating).
