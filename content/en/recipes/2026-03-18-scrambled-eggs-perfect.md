Forget everything you think you know about scrambled eggs. This technique — inspired by Gordon Ramsay — produces the creamiest, most luxurious scrambled eggs you've ever tasted.

## The Cold Start Method

The secret is starting with cold eggs in a cold pan. This sounds counterintuitive, but it allows the proteins to set gradually, creating tiny, silky curds instead of large, rubbery chunks.

## The On-Off Technique

Constantly pulling the pan on and off the heat gives you precise control over the cooking. Think of it as the scrambled egg equivalent of stirring risotto — patience pays off.

## Common Mistakes

- **Too much heat**: High heat = rubber. Always use medium or medium-low
- **Seasoning too early**: Salt breaks down eggs if added before cooking. Season at the end
- **Overcooking**: Remove from heat while still slightly wet. Residual heat finishes the job

## Serving Suggestions

- On thick-cut sourdough with smoked salmon and dill
- In a warm tortilla with avocado and hot sauce
- On an English muffin with crispy bacon
