The chocolate lava cake is the ultimate dinner party flex. Break through the delicate chocolate exterior and a river of molten chocolate flows out. Your guests will think you trained in Paris. In reality, it takes 15 minutes and 8 ingredients.

## The Science of the Molten Center

The lava cake works because the outside bakes and sets while the inside remains liquid. This means timing is everything — a minute too long and you have a regular chocolate cake (still delicious, but not the drama you're after).

## Chocolate Quality Matters

Use the best dark chocolate you can find — at least 70% cocoa. The chocolate IS the dessert, so cheap chocolate means a cheap-tasting cake. Valrhona, Guittard, or Lindt Excellence all work beautifully.

## The Make-Ahead Advantage

Here's the real secret of lava cakes: you can prepare the batter, fill the ramekins, and refrigerate hours ahead. When dessert time comes, just pop them in the oven. This makes them perfect for dinner parties — no last-minute scrambling.

## Temperature Tip

If baking from cold (refrigerated), add 1-2 extra minutes of baking time. Watch for the signs: firm edges, jiggly center.

## Variations

- **Salted caramel center**: Push a frozen salted caramel truffle into the center before baking
- **Espresso**: Add 1 tsp instant espresso to the batter
- **Raspberry**: Place a few fresh raspberries in the center
- **Peanut butter**: Drop 1 tsp peanut butter into each ramekin before baking
