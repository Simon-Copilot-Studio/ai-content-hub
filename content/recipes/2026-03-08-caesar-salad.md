Every great Caesar salad starts with the dressing — and once you make it from scratch, you'll never go back to bottled. The combination of anchovy, garlic, lemon, and Parmesan creates a dressing with more umami and depth than any store-bought version can offer.

## The Dressing Is Everything

The original Caesar salad was invented in 1924 by Caesar Cardini in Tijuana, Mexico. His original dressing was simpler than modern versions, but the principles remain: emulsification, balance, and bold flavors.

### The Umami Base

Anchovies are non-negotiable. Before you skip them — these tiny fillets dissolve completely into the dressing, leaving behind pure savory depth without any fishy taste. Combined with Parmesan (another umami powerhouse) and Worcestershire sauce, you get a triple layer of savory complexity.

### Emulsification

The egg yolk acts as an emulsifier, binding the oil and lemon juice into a creamy, clinging dressing. Drizzle the olive oil in slowly while whisking vigorously — rushing this step results in a broken, oily mess.

### The Acid Balance

Lemon juice provides brightness that cuts through the richness. Dijon mustard adds a subtle heat and helps stabilize the emulsion. Together, they prevent the dressing from tasting heavy.

## Crouton Tips

Homemade croutons take 10 minutes and are vastly better than store-bought:

1. Cut day-old bread (sourdough or ciabatta) into 1-inch cubes
2. Toss with olive oil, garlic powder, salt, and Italian herbs
3. Bake at 375°F for 8-10 minutes until golden and crunchy
4. Cool completely before adding to salad

## Assembly Rules

- **Dry the lettuce completely** — wet leaves dilute the dressing
- **Dress just before serving** — romaine wilts quickly once dressed
- **Use your hands** — tossing by hand ensures every leaf gets coated evenly
- **Shave the Parmesan** — a vegetable peeler creates elegant, large shavings that add visual appeal

## Protein Additions

Turn this side salad into a meal:
- **Grilled chicken breast** — the classic protein pairing
- **Seared shrimp** — toss with lemon and garlic
- **Crispy bacon** — crumbled on top for texture
- **Soft-boiled egg** — halved, for a richer experience
