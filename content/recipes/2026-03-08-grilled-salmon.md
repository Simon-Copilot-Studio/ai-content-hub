There's something magical about perfectly grilled salmon — crisp golden skin on the outside, moist and tender flesh infused with lemon and herbs on the inside. This recipe delivers restaurant-quality results every single time.

## The Three Keys to Perfect Grilled Salmon

### 1. Dry the Fish

This is the most important step that most people skip. Pat each fillet thoroughly with paper towels. Moisture on the surface creates steam instead of sear, which means soggy skin instead of crispy skin.

### 2. High Heat, Don't Move

Place the salmon skin-side down on a properly preheated, well-oiled grill and **don't touch it for 4-5 minutes**. The skin needs sustained contact with heat to render its fat and become crispy. If it sticks, it's not ready — give it another minute.

### 3. Don't Overcook

This is where most home cooks go wrong. Salmon continues to cook for about 5 degrees after you remove it from heat. Pull it at 135°F for perfect medium-rare results. Overcooked salmon is dry and flaky in a bad way.

## The Marinade

The herb-lemon marinade does double duty: it flavors the fish and helps prevent sticking. Only marinate the flesh side — the skin side should stay dry for maximum crispiness. Fifteen to twenty minutes is the sweet spot. Longer than 30 minutes and the acid in the lemon juice starts "cooking" the fish.

## Choosing Your Salmon

- **Wild-caught** (sockeye, king) — deeper flavor, leaner, more expensive
- **Farm-raised** (Atlantic) — milder, fattier, more forgiving to cook
- **Fresh over frozen** — but properly thawed frozen salmon is perfectly fine

Look for fillets with bright, vibrant color and a fresh ocean smell. The flesh should spring back when gently pressed.
