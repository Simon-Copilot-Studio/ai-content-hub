This recipe hits every note — sweet honey, savory soy sauce, aromatic garlic, and the satisfying crunch of perfectly crispy chicken skin. It takes 30 minutes, uses one pan, and tastes like you spent hours in the kitchen.

## The Two Secrets to Perfect Chicken Thighs

### Secret 1: Dry Skin = Crispy Skin

Moisture is the enemy of crispiness. Pat each chicken thigh with paper towels — both sides — until they're bone dry. This might seem obsessive, but it's the single most important step. Wet skin steams instead of crisping.

### Secret 2: Don't Touch It

Place the chicken skin-side down in a hot pan and **walk away for 6-8 minutes**. Every time you move it, you interrupt the crisping process. The fat under the skin needs time to render out, and the skin needs sustained contact with the hot pan to get that glass-like crunch.

You'll know it's ready when the chicken releases easily from the pan. If it sticks, it needs more time.

## Building the Honey Garlic Sauce

The sauce comes together in the same pan, using all those delicious fond (brown bits) left from searing the chicken. This is pure flavor.

### The Ratio

The magic ratio is **equal parts honey and soy sauce** — 1/4 cup each. The honey provides sweetness and caramelization, while soy sauce brings saltiness and umami. Rice vinegar adds a bright, acidic note that keeps the sauce from being cloying.

### Thickening

A cornstarch slurry (1 tbsp cornstarch + 2 tbsp water) transforms the sauce from thin liquid to a glossy, sticky glaze in about 60 seconds. This is what makes the sauce cling to the chicken instead of pooling on the plate.

### The Finish

A drizzle of sesame oil at the very end adds an aromatic, nutty flavor that ties everything together. Don't cook the sesame oil — it should be added off-heat to preserve its flavor.

## Serving Suggestions

**Over rice:** Steamed jasmine rice is the classic choice. The sauce soaks into the rice beautifully.

**With vegetables:** Stir-fried broccoli, steamed bok choy, or roasted green beans all complement the sweet-savory profile.

**Meal prep:** This recipe reheats well. Store chicken and sauce separately — reheat chicken in a 375°F oven for 10 minutes to re-crisp the skin, warm sauce on the stovetop.

## Scaling Up

This recipe doubles easily for meal prep or larger families. Use two skillets or work in batches — overcrowding the pan prevents proper searing. Make a double batch of sauce in one pan while the chicken rests.
