This is the mac and cheese recipe that retired the blue box in our kitchen. One pot, 20 minutes, and it tastes like you spent an hour making a roux and béchamel. The secret? Cook the pasta directly in milk.

## Why One-Pot Works Better

Traditional mac and cheese requires boiling pasta, making a roux, building a béchamel, adding cheese, and combining everything. That's three pots and 45 minutes.

This method combines everything into one pot. As the pasta cooks in milk, it releases starch that naturally thickens the liquid into a creamy sauce — no roux needed. The result is actually *creamier* than the traditional method because every bit of starch stays in the sauce.

## The Cheese Strategy

### The Three-Cheese Formula

Each cheese plays a role:

- **Sharp cheddar (8 oz)** — the flavor backbone. Use aged cheddar for more punch.
- **Gruyère or mozzarella (4 oz)** — adds stretch and melt. Gruyère is more complex; mozzarella is milder and kid-friendlier.
- **Cream cheese (2 oz)** — the secret weapon. Creates a silky, smooth base that prevents the sauce from becoming grainy.

### The Cardinal Rule

**Always shred cheese from a block.** Pre-shredded cheese is coated with cornstarch and cellulose to prevent clumping in the bag — but these same agents prevent smooth melting in your sauce. Block cheese melts into a perfectly smooth, glossy sauce.

### Off-Heat Melting

Remove the pot from heat before adding cheese. Excess heat causes cheese proteins to seize up, creating a grainy, stringy texture. Off-heat, the residual warmth melts the cheese gently into a smooth sauce.

## The Hidden Flavor Boosters

- **Mustard powder** — you won't taste mustard, but it amplifies the cheesy flavor. It's the secret ingredient in nearly every great mac and cheese recipe.
- **Garlic powder** — subtle savory depth
- **Cayenne** — just a pinch adds warmth without noticeable heat

## Upgrades

**Crispy top:** Transfer to an oven-safe dish, top with buttered breadcrumbs and extra cheese, broil 3-4 minutes until golden.

**Bacon mac:** Stir in crumbled crispy bacon and a handful of chives.

**Truffle mac:** Drizzle with truffle oil just before serving.

**Buffalo mac:** Add 2 tbsp hot sauce and top with blue cheese crumbles.

## Kid-Approved, Adult-Worthy

This recipe bridges the gap between the mac and cheese kids crave and the version adults actually want to eat. The Gruyère and mustard powder add sophistication without alienating young palates. For the pickiest eaters, use all cheddar and skip the cayenne.
